## üéØ What We Need You to Test

As a beta tester, we're looking for your feedback on key features and your overall experience. Please focus on the following areas:

### 1. **Patient Onboarding & Management** üè•
**What to test:**
- Adding a new patient with complete intake form
- Taking and attaching baseline wound photos
- Entering patient medical history and consent forms
- Searching and filtering patient lists
- Viewing patient profiles and information

**What to look for:**
- Is the multi-step form easy to follow?
- Are all fields clearly labeled?
- Do photos upload correctly?
- Can you easily find patients later?

### 2. **Session Logging & Wound Tracking** üìä
**What to test:**
- Recording a treatment session for a patient
- Documenting wound measurements (length, width, depth)
- Taking session photos (before/after)
- Recording VAS pain scores (0-10 scale)
- Tracking weight measurements
- Adding session notes

**What to look for:**
- Does the session form capture all necessary information?
- Are wound measurements intuitive?
- Do photos save properly?
- Can you see progress compared to baseline?

### 3. **Multiple Wound Support** ü©π
**What to test:**
- Adding patients with multiple wounds
- Tracking each wound separately
- Recording different measurements for each wound
- Viewing wound-specific progress

**What to look for:**
- Can you easily distinguish between different wounds?
- Does tracking multiple wounds feel organized?
- Is it clear which wound you're documenting?

### 4. **Progress Visualization & Analytics** üìà
**What to test:**
- Viewing patient progress charts (pain, weight, wound healing)
- Checking improvement percentages
- Reviewing treatment timeline
- Comparing before/after photos
- Looking at wound size reduction graphs

**What to look for:**
- Are charts easy to understand?
- Do calculations appear accurate?
- Is progress clearly visible?
- Are trends meaningful?

### 5. **Calendar & Appointments** üìÖ
**What to test:**
- Scheduling new appointments
- Viewing appointments by day/week/month
- Marking appointments as complete
- Adding appointment notes
- Checking appointment reminders

**What to look for:**
- Is scheduling straightforward?
- Can you easily see your schedule?
- Do appointment types make sense?
- Are conflicts detected?

### 6. **Dashboard & Notifications** üîî
**What to test:**
- Viewing dashboard statistics
- Checking real-time notifications
- Reviewing patient improvement alerts
- Accessing recent patient updates
- Quick navigation to patients from dashboard

**What to look for:**
- Is important information easy to find?
- Are notifications helpful and relevant?
- Does the dashboard give a good overview?

### 7. **Reports & AI Features** üìã
**What to test:**
- Generating patient progress reports
- Viewing analytics dashboard
- Using AI-powered report chat (if available)
- Exporting reports as PDF
- Reviewing treatment outcomes

**What to look for:**
- Are reports comprehensive and professional?
- Is AI assistance helpful?
- Do PDFs generate correctly?
- Is data accurate and complete?

### 8. **User Experience & Performance** ‚ö°
**What to test:**
- App responsiveness and speed
- Navigation between screens
- Photo loading times
- Data saving reliability
- Overall design and layout

**What to look for:**
- Does the app feel fast?
- Is navigation intuitive?
- Are there any crashes or freezes?
- Is the design professional and clean?

## üêõ What to Report

We need your honest feedback! Please report:

### Bugs & Issues üî¥
- **Crashes:** App closes unexpectedly

### Usability Feedback üü°

- **Workflow issues:** Steps that feel inefficient
- **Design concerns:** UI elements that don't look right

### Feature Requests üü¢
- **Missing features:** What you wish the app had
- **Improvements:** How existing features could be better
- **Shortcuts:** Faster ways to do common tasks

## üí° Testing Tips

1. **Use realistic data:** Enter data as you would in real practice (but use test patients, not real PHI)
2. **Test edge cases:** Try unusual inputs, skip optional fields, test limits
3. **Try different scenarios:** New patients, follow-ups, multiple wounds, etc.
4. **Test regularly:** Use the app for a few days to spot patterns
5. **Be thorough:** Don't skip features - we need feedback on everything
6. **Be honest:** We want to know what doesn't work well

## ‚ùì FAQ

**Q: Can I use this for real patients?**
A: While the app is functional, it's still in beta. Use test data during beta testing. Check with your organization's policies.

**Q: Will my test data be saved?**
A: Yes, but beta versions may be wiped between major updates. Don't rely on it for critical data yet.

**Q: What if I find a security issue?**
A: Please report it immediately to support@medwave.com with "SECURITY" in the subject line.

**Q: Can I share the app with colleagues?**
A: Yes! Share the download links from this guide. More testers = better feedback!

**Q: How often will updates be released?**
A: We aim for weekly updates during active beta testing based on your feedback.

**Q: What happens after beta testing?**
A: The app will be released to the public App Store/Play Store. Your feedback helps make that happen!
